Case study

Building a reliable data and business intelligence function for a booking platform

The goal is not only to report the numbers, but to make sure the organization can trust what those numbers mean.

Data ManagementBusiness IntelligenceKPI GovernanceSQLData Quality

Context

A booking business connects customer behavior, reservations, nights, revenue, discounts, marketing, supply, and operational processes. These data points often live across multiple systems and reporting cycles.

The challenge

Metrics may be technically calculated while still being misleading if business definitions, source systems, periods, or ownership are not aligned. Booking classifications and identifiers can also create attribution or duplication problems if they are not investigated.

My role

As Data Manager & Business Intelligence, I support product, operations, finance, marketing, and management through KPI governance, data-quality investigations, dashboards, reporting methodologies, and automation.

What I changed

  • Manage recurring KPI reporting and the logic behind key metrics.
  • Use SQL to investigate booking classifications, unmatched identifiers, duplicates, and reporting inconsistencies.
  • Align KPI definitions and calculation approaches across teams and data sources.
  • Build dashboards for bookings, revenue, customers, nights, marketing performance, and daily product health.
  • Automate recurring reporting and reduce manual follow-up.

The main lesson

A dashboard becomes valuable only after the organization agrees on what the metric means, where it comes from, and who owns the response when it changes.

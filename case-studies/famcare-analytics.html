Case study

Building the analytics function inside a growing counseling platform

At Famcare, I was the only data analyst supporting product, operations, technology, quality, marketing, and customer support.

Product AnalyticsOperations AnalyticsMarketing AnalyticsMetabaseSQL

Context

Famcare was a digital counseling platform serving users across mental, social, and family support journeys. Different teams needed reliable answers about product behavior, service operations, counselor quality, campaign performance, retention, and feature adoption.

The challenge

The challenge was not merely producing reports. Data lived across product events, operational processes, marketing platforms, and internal systems. Each team had different questions, and an isolated number could easily be interpreted without the context needed for a good decision.

My role

As the only data analyst, I worked across the full analytical lifecycle: validating whether a problem existed, helping teams define success, preparing measurement requirements, monitoring releases, and checking whether a change produced the intended effect.

What I changed

  • Built live Metabase dashboards for product, operations, quality, marketing, and management.
  • Created validation and error-detection processes that supported 95% data accuracy.
  • Used WebEngage, AppsFlyer, Google Analytics, and Google Ads data to support campaign and conversion analysis.
  • Worked with product teams from problem validation through feature measurement and post-release monitoring.
  • Identified high-value customer segments to support retention and upselling opportunities.
95%data accuracy
+15%dashboard effectiveness
+5%conversion support
80%product goal success

The main lesson

The strongest lesson was that an analyst creates more value when embedded in the decision process—not when waiting at the end for a dashboard request.
